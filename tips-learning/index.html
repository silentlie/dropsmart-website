Tips & Learning

Learn what to fix, before spending anything.

Most households can cut water waste without buying a single device. These guides help you spot what is happening, act on the cheapest fixes first, and decide if a smart meter is actually worth it for your situation.

  • Quick wins with no cost

    Habit changes that work from day one — no tools, no landlord approval.

  • Low-cost fixes that pay back

    Simple hardware upgrades that reduce flow without reducing pressure.

  • A clear upgrade decision guide

    Know when a smart meter will actually answer a question — and when it won't.

How this page works 3 stages
  1. 1

    Fix the obvious first

    Leaks, dripping taps, and habit changes that cost nothing and work immediately. This alone solves most bills.

  2. 2

    Make low-cost upgrades

    Aerators, flow restrictors, and efficient showerheads. Small spend, quick payback, no landlord needed for most.

    Decide on a smart meter

    Only useful once you have a stable baseline and a clear question the data can actually answer.

Basic Principle

Quick wins vs long-term fixes.

Know which actions give results this week, and which ones pay off over months.

Quick wins

Results within days — no cost

  • Shorten daily shower time — works immediately
  • Only run full laundry loads
  • Switch to cold water wash cycle
  • Water garden in early morning only
  • Turn off tap while brushing teeth

Long-term fixes

Results over weeks and months

  • Fix dripping taps and leaking cisterns
  • Install tap aerators and flow restrictors
  • Replace older showerhead with a water-efficient model
  • Set up a smart meter for ongoing tracking
  • Review irrigation system for over-watering zones
