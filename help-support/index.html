FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Quick answers to common questions about DropSmart, the prototype, and how households can use the support information.

What is DropSmart? +

DropSmart is a smart water meter concept designed to help households understand water use, notice possible waste, and make more informed decisions before the next bill arrives.

Is DropSmart a real installation or quoting platform? +

This version is a static planning prototype. It shows how the website could guide users through water-saving decisions, setup preparation, support information, and enquiry forms. It does not provide live quotes, bookings, or real account services.

Who is DropSmart designed for? +

DropSmart is designed for households that want clearer water-use information. This may include homeowners comparing upgrade options, renters preparing questions for landlords, property managers reviewing water-saving ideas, or households trying to reduce waste.

Can DropSmart help me reduce my water bill? +

DropSmart can help users understand where water may be used and where savings may be possible. Actual savings depend on household habits, property type, existing plumbing, local water prices, and whether recommended changes are installed or followed.

Do I need technical knowledge to use DropSmart? +

No. The website is designed to explain water-use information in plain language, with guidance for setup, costs, privacy, and support. The goal is to help users understand the next steps without needing plumbing or technical experience.

Can renters use DropSmart? +

Renters can use DropSmart to learn about water-saving options and prepare questions for landlords or property managers. However, permanent changes such as smart meter installation may require permission from the property owner, landlord, strata body, or manager.

Does DropSmart replace advice from a plumber or water authority? +

No. DropSmart is intended to support planning and education. For installation, safety, plumbing compliance, emergencies, or official water-service requirements, users should contact a licensed professional or their local water authority.
